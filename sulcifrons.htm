masked bee

DETAILSDISTRIBUTION

Each tergum, except, the first has a gradulus which is usually hidden beneath the tergum in front. In Meroglossa species the gradulus of tergum 2 is not hidden. (The plural of tergum is terga.)

The propodeum is the section of the body immediately in front of the constriction. The horizontal upper surface is the area that is diagnostic for females, and the species shown, Meroglossa itamuca, has coarse longitudinal ridges known as rugae.

Meroglossa sulcifrons

    

Size
female 10 mm; male 8.5 mm

Identifying Characteristics
A moderately large species with yellow marking on the back. The male is readily identified by the face marks. The female is very like Meroglossa itamuca but is distinguished by the striate propodeum, which has finer lines than that for Meroglossa itamuca.

Distinctive Features
Like the other Meroglossa species, the female has the gradulus of tergum 2 exposed. The male has white face marks and the depressions of the face contain only small pale areas.
