masked bee

DETAILSDISTRIBUTION

Hylaeus (Prosopisteron) primulipictus

    

Size
female 6.5 mm; male 5 mm

Identifying Characteristics
A small black bee with a silky sheen and a swollen collar, yellow across full width. Female has a completely black face and the male's face is yellow only as far as the antennae.

Distinctive Features
The female can be distinguished from H. trilobatus and H. turgicollaris by the dark patches on the fore wing. The dark marks are less obvious in the wing of the male. The lower face is gently curved, not flat as in H. trilobatus.
